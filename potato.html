Potato Curry Recipe (Aloo Curry)

Tips:

Ingredents:

.1 ⅒ poundsPotatoes (cubed to 3/4 inch for stovetop method, 1 inch to cook in IP)

. 1 ½ cupsOnions (fine chopped or processed)

. 1 cup Tomatoes (chopped or pureed, read notes to use canned tomatoes)

. ½ tablespoon Ginger (fine chopped/grated or paste)

. ½ tablespoon Garlic (fine chopped or paste)

. 1 Green Chilli (slit, optional)

. 12 1-sprigsCurry Leaves (or 1 bay leaf, optional)

. 2 tablespoonsCilantro (coriander leaves/or 1 teaspoon kasuri methi, dried fenugreek)

. 3 tablespoons Oil

. 2 cupsWater (or stock or or light coconut milk, cut down to 1 cup for IP)

.¾ teaspoonSalt (adjust to taste)

. 1 ¼ teaspoonsGaram Masala (adjust to taste)

.1 teaspoonRed Chili Powder (or 1/2 tsp cayenne or 1 tsp paprika)

.¼ teaspoonTurmeric Powder

.¼ teaspoonMustard Seeds (optional)

. ½ teaspoonCumin Seeds (optional)

.1 ½ teaspoonsCoriander Powder (optional)

.½ teaspoonCumin Powder (optional)

instructions:

.Add cooked Potatoes to the Oil and fry till golden. As the Potatoes are changing color, add Asafoetida, Salt, and Pepper Powder and fry till golden.

.In the same pan, heat 2 tbsp of Oil and add Cumin Seeds and Black Cumin, and mix. Add the chopped Onions and fry well.

.When the Onions turn light pink, add the Ginger Garlic Paste and fry.

.Add the Tomato paste and fry till oil separates. Then add the fried Potatoes and fry for 3 minutes.

.Switch off the stove and add the whisked curd and mix well in the Curry. Then switch the stove back on and cook covered on a low flame until the Oil comes to the surface

. Tastes good with White Rice
